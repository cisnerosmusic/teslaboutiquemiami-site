Why South Florida is water-spot country

If your Tesla lives in Miami, water spots are probably the most common cosmetic problem it will face, more than rock chips, more than swirls. The reason is simple: our water is hard and mineral-rich, the humidity is relentless, and the sun bakes whatever lands on the car. Sprinkler overspray in a parking lot, an afternoon thunderstorm followed by full sun, or a wash you did not dry in time all leave the same thing behind: dissolved minerals that stay on the surface after the water evaporates. Near the coast, salt makes it worse.

What a water spot actually is

A water spot is a deposit of minerals, mostly calcium and magnesium, left behind when water dries instead of being wiped away. There are two stages, and the difference matters. Stage one is a surface deposit sitting on top of the paint, glass, film or coating; it wipes off with the right product. Stage two is etching: the mineral has bonded to or eaten into the surface, leaving a ring or crater you can actually feel with a fingertip. Stage one is a five-minute fix. Stage two on paint usually needs machine polishing, which is why catching spots early is everything.

Removing spots from glass

Glass is hard, so it tolerates more, but Tesla glass is often tinted, and that changes the rules. On tinted windows never use abrasive pads, razor blades or ammonia-based cleaners; they damage the film. For light spotting, an anti-static window tint cleaner and a clean microfiber are safe and effective. For heavier deposits on bare glass, a dedicated water-spot remover works: let it dwell, then wipe and buff. The windshield and side glass respond well; just keep any aggressive product off the tint and away from the camera housings.

Removing spots from paint, PPF and ceramic

This is where the right product matters most. XPEL Water Spot Remover is formulated to dissolve mineral deposits safely on paint, XPEL films and FUSION PLUS ceramic coating. Work in the shade, never under direct sun: shake the bottle, spray onto the spot, let it sit 30 to 45 seconds, then wipe with a clean microfiber and buff. Repeat once if needed. Do not scrub with anything abrasive, and do not reach for vinegar or household descalers on a coated or filmed car; they can strip or dull your protection. If a spot will not budge and you can feel a ring with your fingertip, it has etched, and that needs paint correction, not more rubbing.

Why a coated Tesla barely spots at all

Here is the upside of protection. A ceramic coating is hydrophobic, so water beads up and rolls off instead of sheeting and drying in place, which means far fewer deposits form to begin with. And when spots do form, they sit on the slick coating rather than bonding to bare paint, so they release with a quick wipe. The same is true over PPF. Keeping the hydrophobics fresh with XPEL Ceramic Boost every few washes keeps that self-cleaning behavior working.

How to stop them coming back

Prevention beats removal every time:

  • Never let water dry on the car. Dry completely with clean microfiber after every wash or rain.
  • Wash early or in the shade, never under the midday Miami sun.
  • Avoid sprinkler zones when you park; reclaimed irrigation water is especially mineral-heavy.
  • Keep a coating on the car so deposits cannot bond, and refresh it with Ceramic Boost.
  • Act fast on any spot; surface deposits are easy, etched ones are not.
