Good news first: you chose the low-maintenance path

XPEL film heals its own light scratches with heat (and Miami sun activates that for free), and FUSION PLUS ceramic makes dirt let go with far less effort. So this is not a list of chores. It is a short set of habits that protect your protection, because in South Florida the real enemies are not rock chips anymore. They are water that dries in the sun, and the acidic stuff that lands on your car and sits there.

Habit 1: wash by hand, with the right soap

Wash with a pH-neutral shampoo, a clean microfiber mitt and the classic two-bucket method. Skip brush-style automatic tunnels: those spinning brushes drag grit across the surface and can catch film edges. Touchless is acceptable in a pinch, but hands and microfiber win. And one Tesla-specific tip: before you start, turn on Car Wash Mode from the screen, so the windows close, the wipers stay off and the charge port stays locked.

Habit 2: never let water dry on the car

This is the single most important habit in Miami. Sprinkler overspray, an afternoon thunderstorm followed by full sun, or a wash you did not dry: they all leave mineral deposits that etch into any surface, and water spots are the one thing no film or coating warranty covers. Wash early in the morning or in the shade, never under midday sun, and always dry completely with clean microfiber. If spots already set, XPEL Water Spot Remover dissolves them in under a minute; spray, wait 30 to 45 seconds, wipe. Our full guide to removing water spots from glass and paint walks through it step by step.

Habit 3: do not let the organic stuff sit

Love bug season, bird droppings under that shady ficus, tree sap: all of it is acidic, and Florida heat accelerates the etching. The film takes the hit instead of your paint, but the sooner you clean it, the better the film looks long term. Keep XPEL Detail Spray and a microfiber in the trunk for exactly this; for tougher residue like tar or baked-on bugs, XPEL PPF Cleaner restores the film without harming it.

Habit 4: recharge the hydrophobics

Every few washes, follow up with XPEL Ceramic Boost. It tops up the water beading and gloss on FUSION PLUS, and works on the film too. Five minutes after a wash, and the next three washes get easier. If your Tesla wears STEALTH satin film, remember the one rule: nothing that adds gloss, no wax, no polish.

Habit 5: once a year, let us look at it

Twice a year if you park outdoors near the coast, where salt air works faster. We inspect the film edges, decontaminate embedded brake dust with iron remover, check the coating's behavior and refresh what needs refreshing. It is the cheapest insurance on the investment you already made.

One note for fresh installs

In the first days after installation, give the film time to cure: no washing for about a week, and do not worry if you see small moisture pockets or a light haze. That is water from installation evaporating through the film, and it clears on its own. We will tell you exactly what is normal when you pick up the car.
